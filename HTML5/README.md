# Web Accessibility Site Developed in MadCap Flare
For my final project in English 381, I put together this website using MadCap Flare featuring information on web accessibility with a focus on making accessible video content. The website offers an in-depth analysis of web accessibility and practical guidance on implementing accessibility features when developing video content.

## Objectives
- Demonstrate my understanding of **web accessibility**, a crucial topic for any web developer or content creator, to potential employers :accessibility:
- Showcase my ability to utilize **MadCap Flare** to create web content, increasing the tools I am proficient with 🛠️

## About this Flare Project
The first section, titled "Web Accessibility," gives a summary of disability, explains multiple approaches to web accessibility, and highlights the importance of universal design. This section was developed by me individually using readings and classwork done in my English 381 course. The second section, titled "Video Accessibility: Closed Captions," defines closed captions, articulates their importance to accessibility, offers a brief history of closed captions, and gives step-by-step instructions for implementing closed captions into videos on Microsoft's Clipchamp. This section was created collobaratively within a small team of classmates.

### About My Team
For the video accessibility part of this project, my classmates and I were split into teams to dive more deeply into specific aspects of video accessibility. My team was tasked with exploring **closed captions**. Besides myself, my team included:
- Jane Edison
- Zoe Rakarich

As a team, we focused on coming up with a functional definition of closed captions and explaining how important they are to ensuring videos are accessible, especially for those with auditory disabilities. We also made a brief timeline to highlight the development of closed captions into the widely available feature they are today. Finally, we developed some instructional modules to guide users through the process of adding and editing closed captions on Clipchamp, Microsoft's video recording/editing software.

We decided to split up the workload based on the content pages. Jane focused on the definition and importance of closed captions. Zoe created the timeline displaying the history of closed captions. I documented Clipchamp and created the step-by-step instructional pages on adding and editing closed captions.

### Tools and Concepts
In the making of this website, I utilized MadCap Flare's **XML and HTML Text Editor** tools to put together the topic pages. I also documented **Clipchamp** using **Google Docs** before moving the content into MadCap Flare.
